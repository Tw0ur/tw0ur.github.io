4-lecture The concept of knowledge in the teachings of Abai. The importance of "knowledge and education" in the teachings of Abay and his system. TAXONOMY OF ABAY
Abai's teaching contains a holistic hierarchical system of psychology of cognitive processes, such as perception, demand, knowledge, understanding what you know, remembering what you understand, thinking, using. This system has not been specially studied and defined in line with the teachings of Abai. For independent Kazakhstan, it is vital to determine an integral hierarchical system of the psychology of the cognitive process in the teachings of Abai, such as perception, requirement, knowledge, understanding, memorization, thinking, application and develop a scientific and methodological basis and theoretical mechanisms for its application.
Of great social importance is the creation of a scientific basis and a mechanism for applying the teachings of Abay as a non-alternative spiritual value, as a subject at all levels of education and upbringing and in all spheres of public life.In the overwhelming majority of his works, both in poetry and in prose, even in riddles, Abay calls on the Kazakhs to acquire knowledge.
Taxonomy of Abai.
The taxonomy of Abai is the most important value of the domestic theory of learning, the theoretical basis of educational activities and research work specially carried out at the interdisciplinary level in the field of psychology of cognitive processes in learning. In the concept of education of Abai there is the concept of taxonomy of Abai. It is a hierarchical system of cognitive processes based on the concept of Abay's education. The system is built on six levels based on the logic used in the concept of education:
DESIRE → PERCEPTION → KNOWLEDGE → UNDERSTANDING → MEMORY → APPLICATION.
These levels, reflected in the teachings of Abai, are accepted and proposed in their original form, according to the model shown by Abai.
When modeling the taxonomy of Abai, a model was chosen that is suitable for describing the levels of the cognitive process.
